Title: Dreams of a New Day: Songs by Black Composers (CofC Int’l. Piano Series)

Liaison: Kristin Alexander

Date: April 19 (Sottile Theatre)

Internationally recognized operatic baritone Will Liverman and acclaimed pianist and CofC's Director of Piano Studies Paul Sánchez will debut to Charleston their highly praised, Billboard chart-topping album, Dreams of a New Day: Songs by Black Composers. Recently nominated for a Grammy, the album highlights Black composers across generations, from early 20th-century pioneers to contemporary composers. Presented by the Quattlebaum Artist in Residence Fund.  Join some of the artists for a pre-concert talk, including Charleston's first Poet Laureate Marcus Amaker who wrote the text for part of the song, "Two Black Churches.” The piece reflects on the 16th Street Baptist Church bombing in Birmingham, Alabama (1963) and the Mother Emanuel AME Church shooting in Charleston (2015). The pre-concert talk will begin at 7:30pm, with the concert at 8:00pm. FREE ADMISSION for CofC students, faculty & staff, and youth (18 & under). All attendees must obtain tickets.
